NOW YOU CAN SPECIFY MORE DETAILS FOR YOUR STUDY AREA

Please insert the data for the four categories: species, study area, simulation and anthropogenic acitivities. Remember that the species section was automatically filled while chosing the species before. All of them have default values to ease the process. For more information, you can explore the information button. You will be able to return and change the inputs once you finished the simulation.


Species
Life expectancy: years
Value of the average life expectancy. The algorithm uses a normal distribution around this value with standard deviation of 10 percent.

Age maturation: between and years
Interval of age maturation. The juvenile individuals become adults between these ages following a normal distribution.

Age menopause: years
Approximate age in which females stop getting pregnant.

Pregnancy cycle: between and years
Time between two calves birth, including gestation, lactation and rest.

Probability of pregnancy: %
The probability of a female having an offspring in the following year by a binomial distribution. It is applied only to females that are not pregnant, resting after pregnancy, or sterile.

Probability of survival for adults: %
Probability of surviving for adult individuals determined by a binomial distribution.

Probability of survival for juveniles: %
Probability of surviving for juvenile individuals determined by a binomial distribution. It should be, in general, lower than the probability for adults.
Study area
Initial population: individuals

Percentage of juveniles: % of juveniles
The juvenile individuals will be 50% male and 50% female.

Percentage of adult females: % of females

Number of ships crossing the study area at the first year: ships

Estimated number of ships crossing the study area at the last year: ships
It can be higher, equal or lower than the initial number of ships
Simulation
Time simulated: years

Number of simulations: simulations
How many times this scenario is simulated. It is important to avoid random results but keep it still aleatory. For a 100 interactions loop = 5s; 500 = 15s; 1000 = 20s. More than 1000 simulations might not affect the results.
Anthropogenic Threats
Ship strike ratio: strikes per year ships per strike
The data can be based on a year basis or ship basis. It is recommended to use a ratio based on data or estimations from the study area. Also, it is strongly recommended to use the ship basis when the amount of ships increase over time. Values do not need to be round.

Category of individuals affecteded by ship strikes:
An worst case scenario can be predicted by selecting the most sensitive class of the population, in this case females or juveniles.

Male, female, and juvenile
Only Female
Only Juvenile

Whaling or Bycatch: whales fished per years
If the study area allows whaling, estimations of individuals hunted in the group can be added. It can assume non-round values.

Stranding or beaching: whales found on the beach per year
It occurs when whales swim or float to shore and cannot remove themselves. It is associated to sick animals, injured, navigational errors or social bonding too close to the shore. It can assume non-round values.

Other threats: whales extracted per year
In case the study area faces a threat that was not predicted, the amount of individuals to affected and removed each year can be inserted here. It can assume non-round values.
