SMMR

SIMULATION OF MARINE MAMMALS RESILIENCE

Welcome to an online application designed to evaluate the impact of anthropogenic activities on marine mammals species

HOW IT WORKS SIMULATE

OUR GOAL

TO BETTER UNDERSTAND HOW HUMAN ACTIVITIES AFFECT MARINE MAMMALS

Measuring how public policies and regional regulations may affect the ecosystem remains a challenge for several marine protected areas, creating difficulties for competent authorities to implement new actions, which may affect the acceptance among the stakeholders. Despite being a difficult task, understanding the drivers behind animal population fluctuations is key to conservation, ecology, and wildlife management.

HOW IT WORKS

The method applied is based on mathematical simulations derived from agent-based models and population dynamics that can estimate how a certain population will behave throughout time by simulating a number of individuals and their main stages of life under the pressure of external factors.

First, insert the data concerning the region you are analyzing. Estimations of the external stressor are necessary, preferably based on historical data.

The model is very sensitive to the species select and their life parameters. Choose one of the standard species and the parameters will be automatically updated based on the literature, otherwise, you can model your own species.

The simulation will run and it might take a few minutes if the number of repetitions is increased. To eliminate the randomness embedded in real life cases, the simulation is run several times and 5% of the extreme cases are discharged.

After the simuation is over, the results will be obtained by calculating the average among all the scenarios obtained. Also, the probability for different scenarios is calculated, as well as the standard deviation.

The graphs shown in the output section will help the user to better understand how the population may fluctuate in the next years. Moreover, they help to understand the stability based on the age profile and estimated worst-case scenario.

In order to better understand how the simulation is working, a flowchart summarises the main events. After the data is inserted by the user, two main loops occur. The first will repeat events such as death and birth over every year of the time period simulated, while the second will restart the simulation from the first year to compute several possibilities for the same starting scenario.

Moreover, this application is open-source and users are free to download it and adapt code to specific to more specific scenarios or improve current features. The code is available here.
