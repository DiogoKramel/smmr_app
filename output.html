Through this line chart, we can visualize the trend over time (in years). The chart shows the total population variation throughout time, as well as divided into three categories: adult females, adult males, and juveniles of both genders. The confidence interval for the total population limits is 99%.


Here we can see the distribution of the ship strikes and natural deaths observed in the time period simulated, as well as other threats, in case of any. The numbers per year are an approximation of the average, and for this reason, they are not necessarily round numbers.

The graph below may help you understand the aging process of the population. In case they happen to be growing older on average, the fertility will decrease and in a near future population will also decrease.

The same applies to the age profile in the last year. Normally, the population will have a high number of juveniles, that slowly decrease until the life expectancy is reached.

A probability density function (PDF) is a function, whose value can be interpreted as providing a relative likelihood for a certain number of collisions. The sum of each individual probability should result in 100%.

Cumulative distribution function (CDF) of the probability of having a certain number of deaths, is the probability for having that number of deaths or less. Choose the cause of death in the dropdown box to update the graph.

Lastly, all the simulations run are plotted over time, along with the standard deviation and the confidence interval, in order to show the most positive and negative cases and how far they are from the average.
